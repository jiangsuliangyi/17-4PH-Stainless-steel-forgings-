A detailed technical overview of **17-4PH (AISI 630)**, a martensitic precipitation-hardening stainless steel renowned for its unique combination of high strength, excellent mechanical properties, and good corrosion resistance.

---

## 🔍 Overview

**17-4PH** (UNS S17400 / 1.4542) is the most widely used precipitation-hardening grade. The "17-4" refers to its typical content of **17% Chromium** and **4% Nickel**. With the addition of copper, this alloy can be hardened through a simple, low-temperature heat treatment process, making it an ideal choice for high-performance forged components.

### Key Characteristics:
*   **High Strength:** Yield strength up to 1100-1300 MPa (160-190 ksi) in the H900 condition.
*   **Corrosion Resistance:** Comparable to 304 stainless steel in most environments.
*   **Versatility:** Mechanical properties can be optimized by adjusting the aging temperature.
*   **Excellent Machinability:** Readily fabricated and machined in the solution-annealed state.

## ⚙️ Mechanical Properties (Typical)

The properties of 17-4PH forgings vary significantly depending on the **Heat Treatment (Aging)** condition:

| Condition | Tensile Strength (MPa) | Yield Strength (MPa) | Elongation (%) | Hardness (HRC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **H900** | ≥ 1310 | ≥ 1170 | ≥ 10 | 40 - 47 |
| **H1025** | ≥ 1070 | ≥ 1000 | ≥ 12 | 35 - 42 |
| **H1100** | ≥ 965 | ≥ 795 | ≥ 14 | 31 - 37 |
| **H1150** | ≥ 930 | ≥ 725 | ≥ 16 | 28 - 35 |

## 🏗️ Common Applications

Due to its high reliability and strength-to-weight ratio, 17-4PH forged parts are critical in:
*   **Aerospace:** Structural parts, engine components, and fasteners.
*   **Oil & Gas:** Valve internals, pump shafts, and offshore equipment.
*   **Chemical Processing:** Agitators, mixer shafts, and reactor components.
*   **Medical:** Surgical instruments and orthopedic devices.
*   **Marine:** Propeller shafts and high-load marine fittings.

## 🛠️ Forging & Heat Treatment

*   **Forging:** Typically performed between **1150°C and 1200°C (2100°F - 2200°F)**. Forgings should be air-cooled to room temperature to ensure a complete martensitic transformation.
*   **Solution Annealing (Cond A):** Heating to 1040°C (1900°F) followed by rapid cooling.
*   **Aging:** Hardening is achieved by heating at specific temperatures (e.g., 482°C for H900) for 1–4 hours.
